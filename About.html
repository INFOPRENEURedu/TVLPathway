Technical-Vocational-Livelihood

The Technical-Vocational-Livelihood (TVL) track was formally integrated into the Philippine basic education system through the Enhanced Basic Education Act of 2013 (Republic Act 10533), which later on established the K–12 program on Cavite National Highschool Senior Highschool (CNHS-SHS). Aimed at addressing the "jobs-skills mismatch," the TVL track was designed to provide senior high school students with practical, industry-aligned competencies in sectors such as Home Economics, Industrial Arts, and ICT. When the Senior High School (SHS) program was fully rolled out in 2016, the TVL track became a vital pathway for students seeking immediate employment or entrepreneurship through TESDA National Certifications (NC), while still remaining a viable route to higher education. Over the years, the track has evolved from being perceived as a secondary option to a respected career choice, though it continues to navigate challenges like resource limitations and the need for stronger industry partnerships to ensure graduates are truly "work-ready."
